Cybertruck Robotic Cell Re-Design

Role: Equipment Engineering Intern @ Tesla

Problem: A key robotic cell on the Cybertruck production line was improperly disposing of rejected seat structures, dropping them on the floor. This resulted in hundreds of thousands of dollars in annual material scrap, frequent production downtime for manual retrieval, and created a safety hazard for associates entering the cell.

My Solution: As the sole owner of this project, I engineered a complete mechanical and robotics solution. I designed a custom, non-intrusive rejection chute from the ground up using Solidworks and Siemens NX, ensuring it integrated seamlessly into the existing cell's tight footprint. I then single-handedly reprogrammed the entire Fanuc robotic logic, rewriting the pathing to flawlessly deposit rejected parts into the new assembly.

Results: I drove the project through the full lifecycle, leading a cross-functional team of stakeholders from manufacturing, compliance, and testing to successfully implement my design. The solution completely eliminated the scrap issue and associated downtime, **delivering an annual savings of hundreds of thousands of dollars** while improving cell safety and maintaining the original workflow for production associates.

Helicopter Landing Thermal Analysis (CFD)

Role: Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) Intern @ Lockheed Martin

Problem: The Canadian Surface Combatant (CSC) program needed to validate safe helicopter landing conditions, but it was unknown how hot turbine exhaust would affect the flight deck under dozens of different headwind angles, engine throttles, and outtake arrangements. The team lacked the critical thermal data required for these RANS simulations.

My Solution: I developed the foundational thermodynamic model to solve this problem. I first calculated the previously unknown engine exhaust mass flow rates and temperatures for all required scenarios. Using this data, I developed, meshed, and analyzed the full set of 28 CFD simulations in ANSYS Fluent, modeling all combinations of headwind angles and engine uptake conditions.

Results: My simulations generated 28 thermal contour maps showing the maximum temperature over the flight deck for every possible condition. I consolidated this complex data into a single, concise 'logic board' that was delivered to vendors, enabling them to validate safe helicopter landing envelopes for the multi-billion dollar program.

Production Line Data Automation

Role: Aerospace Engineering Intern @ RTX (Pratt & Whitney)

Problem: Strategic decisions for optimizing the PW800 production line were dependent on a highly inefficient, manual data-pulling process. Multiple times a year, three engineers would spend two full days (a 48 person-hour task) manually extracting and formatting live turbine blade data from SAP, a process that was both slow and prone to human error.

My Solution: Tasked as one of the manual headcounts, I instead took the initiative to single-handedly engineer a permanent automated solution. I developed a complex Visual Basic script that interfaces directly with SAP, loops through hundreds of processes, and automatically transforms the raw data. The script features robust error handling and outputs a concise, executive-ready spreadsheet for immediate analysis.

Results: My script completely **eliminated the 48 person-hour manual process**, saving hundreds of engineering hours annually and significantly increasing data accuracy. The solution was resiliency-tested and implemented as the new company standard. The report I automated now serves as the baseline for all strategic optimization decisions, and I created clear documentation for any engineer to use and maintain it going forward.
