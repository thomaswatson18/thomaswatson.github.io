EV Drag Reduction Analysis @ Lucid Motors

Role: Mechanical Design Engineering Intern

Problem: The design team needed to reduce the aerodynamic drag of a new side-mirror housing by 5% to meet energy efficiency targets, but physical prototypes were too costly for initial iteration.

My Solution: I developed a high-fidelity CFD model in COMSOL to simulate airflow around 10 different housing geometries. I automated the meshing and post-processing scripts in Python, which allowed for rapid evaluation.

Results: My simulations identified a novel geometry that reduced drag by 8.2%, exceeding the target. This design was adopted for wind-tunnel testing, saving an estimated $20,000 in prototyping costs.
